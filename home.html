RDK is a carefully designed system of 3D printed parts that connect with commonly available 1/2" PVC pipe to support robot design. The core question of RDK is simple: how can we make conversations about robots more generative and exploratory? RDK is inspired by hands-on computing and robotics educational materials as well as construction kits (i.e. LEGO, K'NEX, Lincoln Logs, etc).

RDK aims to connect engineering experts and end-users / stakeholders of robotic systems. While these two groups often lack a shared familiarity with or language in mechanical engineering and computer science, they both have important and valuable insights to share. RDK creates a shared medium for expressing ideas and building understanding. RDK centers the idea that, by strengthening generative collaboration with robot users / stakeholders, we can build a robot future that is more flexible, understanding, and responsive.

Robot Development Kit Goals :

  • low cost / complexity
    • RDK has no electronics, it's simple and affordable to print
    • Materials are cheap and easy to access, built with FDM 3D printing and 1/2" PVC pipe
  • teach stakeholders about robots as they build
    • RDK prioritizes open exploration and simplicity, making it fun and easy to start creating robot designs
    • RDK encourages users to tinker and hack as they hammer, twist, and tighten parts
  • enable extensibility and customization
    • RDK is well documented, open source, and prioritizes components, making it easy to add one part without re-designing everything
    • RDK's size pairs well with common ideation materials like pen and paper, so imagining new parts can be as simple as attaching a note
