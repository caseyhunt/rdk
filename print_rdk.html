print your own robot development kit

0.

rdk requires a few simple parts

Beyond 3D printed pieces, RDK requires a small handful of purchased parts.

  • 1/2" PVC Pipe and pipe cutters
  • M4 heat set inserts, 6mm length
  • M4 screws, 3/4" long (hex head recommended)

1.

test your printer tolerance

For the robot development kit to work well, correct tolerances are very important. Establishing a baseline measurement for the tolerance of your 3D printer will make things easier for you. To measure your print tolerance, you can use one of many models provided free online. I've provided a few suggestions below, or you can use the one that you prefer. It is important to test your printer using the parameters that you will use while printing the robot development kit. So, for example, if you want to print the kit at 150% speed, you should also print the tolerance test with that same setting.

  • tolerance test by zapta on thingiverse
  • tolerance tester by eliobou on printables
  • tolerance tester 2.0 by jpdesign on printables

2.

download your starter pack

Once you have an idea of your printer tolerance, you can download one of the zip files below! These files are meant to serve as a starting point for printer tolerances that seem common on consumer FDM printers. If your print tolerance is not represented, you can jump to step 4 (fine tune your fit). Unfortunately, there is no guarantee that the parts provided in the .zip file work correctly with your printer. Instead, this is meant to be a starting point to make it easier for you.

  • 0.3 mm tolerance RDK download
  • 0.5 mm tolerance RDK download

3.

print a test

The parts are designed to be printed with the cap using bridging (no supports). While this is not necessary, it makes preparing parts much easier. Whatever you choose, you should repeat the same parameters across the entire kit. It is strongly recommended to print a couple of pieces first, especially a coupler and a static connector cap. You should check that these have a comfortable amount of grip on a 1/2" PVC pipe, and also interlock with each other tightly. If tests go well, then great! You can proceed. If not, you may need to fine tune your fit (step 4).

4.

fine tune your fit

To fine tune your fit, it is recommended to establish two measurements through test prints: the ideal size of caps and the ideal tolerance of the tongue of the tongue and groove connectors. Pick one or two caps to use to test these values before applying these same measurements across the kit. Once you know the two measurements work for you, the rest should be easy!

5.

design your own parts

After using the kit, I hope that you are inspired to add more parts. Customizing RDK to your own use case allows for even more creativity! Creating your own parts is very simple, with the most challenging part being the tongue-and-groove design. I include the measurements here for a 0.5 mm tolerance design, so you will need to adjust them to your own fit!
